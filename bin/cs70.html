4. Stable trios: 8 points.
Suppose there are n men, n women, and n pet dogs that we want to group into trios of one man, woman, and
dog each. The men and women have preference lists for each other as in the usual stable marriage problem.
In addition, the men and dogs have preference lists for each other. However, each woman has a set of dogs
that she hates.
We want to nd a way to group the men, women, and dogs into trios of one man, one woman, and one dog
each such that the following stability criteria hold:
1. No man and dog not in the same trio prefer each other to their dog and man in their respective trios
2. Each man is in a trio with the best dog he can get in any trio satisfying condition 1
3. No man and woman not in the same trio prefer each other to their woman and man in their respective
trios
4. No woman is in a trio with a dog that she hates
Show how you can nd a stable trio if one exists, and otherwise determine that there is no stable trio.
